Purpose of this WebSite

This website is primarily intended for people whose native language is not Japanese to use when learning kanji.
Please make use of this site and deepen your knowledge of kanji while having fun.

Rules

When you select "Kanji Quiz" and start the game, you will be given 10 kanji questions per round.
In this quiz, please enter the reading of the kanji and submit it.
You can answer in Japanese or in the Roman alphabet. After answering all 10 questions,
your score will be displayed at the end.
"Review" is still being implemented, so stay tuned! The ranking board below records each user's highest score.
5 players from 1st to 5th place are displayed.

Points to Note

When answering questions about the reading of kanji in romaji, there are a few things to keep in mind.
Even if you think you have entered the correct kanji reading, if the romanization is incorrect it will be incorrect.
This is because the readings of the kanji are entered in the Kunrei system. The correct reading should be entered in the Hepburn system.
If you would like to learn about the Romanization format, visit the following page. Recommended form of romanization of Japanese (University of Tokyo)
Below are some examples of Hepburn style:

  • The correct answer is "shi" but you enter "si"
  • The correct answer is "tsu" but you enter "tu"
  • The correct answer is "fu" but you enter "hu"
  • The correct answer is "zyu" but you enter "ju"
  • The correct answer is "ji" but you enter "zi"
  • The correct answer is "sho" but you enter "syo"
